Greetings from Buenos Aires!

Aires Bed & Breakfast Hotel Postcard

Illustrating a postcard meant to be a branding asset for a hotel located in Buenos Aires.

  • Project Type: Assignment
  • Completed: March 2019
  • Tools used: Adobe Illustrator

Creative Strategy: The goal of this project was to design and illustrate a postcard as a branding asset for an assigned hotel. I was assigned a Bed & Breakfast hotel located in Buenos Aires that is targeted towards millenials in the area. It was specifically requested that the illustration on the postcard should include elements representating Buenos Aires culture and the hotel bed and breakfast type. When planning for this project, I did research on the city to find out what symbols, colours, and other elements to use. My biggest source of inspiration were the buildings in the La Boca area that are painted in bold, bright blocks of colours such as pink, blue, and yellow. I believe using bright and colourful imagery in the branding would be reflective of vibrant Buenos Aires culture as well as be attractive to a millenial target market.


With inspiration from the bold colours of La Boca, I decided on these 4 main colours as the palette for the illustration. In order to create consistency and harmony throughout the piece, I limited myself to only these 4 colours and their tints. This would avoid having too many different colours within the piece and allows me to have a greater focus on the actual composition. I also believe minimizing the colour variation allows for a more harmonious and balanced piece. For the font, I decided to go with something minimal but that gave off a slight fun and whimsical feeling.

The Results:




I decided to go with a stylized illustration of Buenos Aires landscape and architecture. When researching the city, I was attracted to the mix of both modern and traditional buildings and how they all came together to create a beautiful and unique cityscape. I chose to make the foreground the La Boca buildings to be the main point of interest in the illustration because I felt that it would be the most attractive area within Buenos Aires for millenial travelling visitors and that the bright colours and shapes would best represent the vibrant culture and city life in Buenos Aires. The background consists of silhouettes of tall city buildings, both business and traditional, including the Obelisca tower which is a recognizable landmark within the city. The curtains on the either side of the illustration are indication that the viewer is looking at this scene from the window of the hotel as they wake up in the morning (represented by the rising sun). This illustration is meant to show potential hotel customers what they can expect to wake up to by staying in the heart of the city and the experiences it has to offer all around them.

I decided on going with a geometric semi-flat illustration style. I felt that the geometric design would be a fun way to showcase the colour-blocking of the La Boca buildings and would create cohesiveness and unity with the shapes of the buildings themselves.
